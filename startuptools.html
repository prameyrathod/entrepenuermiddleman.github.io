Top 5 handy and useful tools for startups and small business

1. Excel spreadsheets

Chron.com reveals that most businesses often use Excel, a Microsoft spreadsheet application often installed by default as part of the Microsoft Office suite on business computers. Ever since its inception, Microsoft Excel has grown to become arguably the most significant computer program in workplaces around the world. With a host of useful built-in functionality and user defined functions, it works like a mini accounting and finance management software. Be it budgeting, organizing reports, or planning a social gathering, Excel is one of the primary tools used by businesses worldwide.

2. Shift Planning

Running a small business is a complicated task. And keeping a track of staff’s shift timings, managing their workflows and leaves, scheduling their shift changes are just a few activities that consume most of the startups resources and time. While most businesses still rely on spreadsheets to manage shifts and vacations, others have turned to online scheduling tools designed to make the process simpler. One such tool is ShiftPlanning.com. This service helps you create and share schedules, manage vacation and shift requests, and send alerts via email and text message, reveals Inc.com. And if you have time on your hands, then go for its mobile app that allows you to manage these activities on the go.

Manage your shift timing.

3.Google AdWords

Now, who hasn’t heard about Google AdWords! It offers assured, immediate traffic to websites against a little investment. Therefore, using AdWords comes with a host of benefits for startups and small businesses. Remember, Google is the largest search engine on the Internet. And if you wish to take your business to the next level, you need to smartly use Google to your advantage. AdWords makes boost your marketing efforts and brings huge per click traffic to your site through the use of proper keywords. And when these keywords translate to sales revenue, the traffic sourced with the help of Google AdWords is converted to profit.

4.Buffer

Every business understands that it’s social media that can take the elements of their brand and showcase those elements to the world in a way that’s even more awesome. But how do you effectively take elements of your business to social media? The answer is simple; you use Buffer! It is perhaps the best way to drive traffic, increase fan engagement and save time on social media. You can use Buffer to share your content on the social media to entertain your followers and also see what’s working and what’s not through its built-in analytics. It efficiently resolves several problems faced by startups and small businesses. Some of the most prominent ones are:

    -problems of being active on social media sites like Twitter,    Facebook and LinkedIn
    -problem of easily sharing content from webpage across all popular social media channels.
    -The problem of scheduling specific days and times and customization of messages.

5. Pivotal Tracker

Though management of work flow in a small business is not that typical, it demands a lot of efforts and time. It is where Pivotal Tracker comes to rescue. It can be of great help to small businesses who find it difficult to manage their workflow. Pivotal Tracker allows:

-Prioritization and re-prioritization of the order of the tasks
-Tracking of progress and easy communication of changes.
-Transparent scheduling and reporting so that entire team is aware of their work and deadlines to be met.

This sprint planning tool is available in multiple forms: from free version to different levels for teams and can be chosen depending upon the needs and sizes of the teams.
